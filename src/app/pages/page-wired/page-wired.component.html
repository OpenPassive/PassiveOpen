Smart home

In addition to being a regular home, this house is also a smart home that employs a lot of domotics. This involves the use of many sensors and lights that can be controlled remotely. Wired -cloudless- connections are typically more reliable and affordable than wireless options such as Wi-Fi, which means that the setup may differ from what you're familiar with.

Electricity

Normal - 230v

In Europe (230v), sockets usually share a breaker or a group that is rated at either 16 or 10 amperes. The rating depends on the load and wire thickness, which is 2.5mm2 and 1.5mm2, respectively.

3Phase - 400v

The unusual symbol you're seeing is a perilex plug, which typically contains three phases, though some models may only have two. The main objective of this plug is to distribute the load over three wires, rather than one, which requires the use of more wires. This type of plug is commonly used for high-power appliances such as induction cookers in the kitchen. Electric cars do not utilize this plug, but they do require three-phase power in most cases.

lightning

Lamps

Lamp outlets are available in both 230V and 24V variants. Typically, most lamps can be dimmed, and their wiring runs back to the server room. They are controlled by a hub that is connected to the home automation system.

Double switch

The double pulse switches have six different options available. You can program actions for two single presses, two double presses, and two long presses. The switches operate at 24V, and their wiring runs back to the hub .

Dimmer

The dimmer switch enables you to adjust the brightness of the room's lighting. However, the smart-home system should automatically select the appropriate dimming level based on the time of day, see circadian lightning.. You can override this automation by using the dimmer switch.

DLC, or light-socket

To outsiders, it may seem strange, but each fixed light in the house has its own separate mini-socket. In Sweden, an old standard was in use, but since 2019, they have followed the EU standard of DLC.

Network / Ethernet

Ethernet (rj45)

Reliable internet is essential when working from home, and the best option is to have a wall outlet for a regular internet cable. These outlets are not limited to computers and can be used for other devices like speakers, televisions, and more.

Power-over-Ethernet

With a voltage of 48 volts, internet cables can be used to power certain devices without requiring additional wiring. This capability makes them a convenient option for devices like ring doorbells, security cameras, and Wi-Fi access points.

Wifi

Needs no introduction. Know that you can have more complex wifi solutions than your internet provider can provide.

Safety

Smoke/heat detectors

For safety purposes, it is advisable to install several smoke sensors throughout the house, along with a heat sensor specifically in the kitchen.

Camera's

While not necessary, installing a security monitoring system in your home can offer added convenience and security. With your own VPN, you can remotely monitor your house from anywhere.

Window/Door sensors/Motion detection

The alarm function is expanded and fortified with window/door sensors and motion detection to provide added security.

Other smarts

Sun blinds / screens

To protect against high sun radiation in summer, a passive house must be equipped with sun blinds/screens. The screens can be passive or automatically controlled by the smart-home system. External screens are more effective than internal screens.

Motion sensors

Motion sensors can be a fun addition to your smart-home setup, allowing for automated control of lights turning on and off. They can also enhance home security by supporting the alarm system.

Temperature

Temperature sensors play a important role in regulating the heating, cooling, and ventilation in specific areas of the house.

Ventilation

Ventilation in

Due to the airtightness of the house, fresh air is necessary in the bedrooms and main living rooms to ensure proper ventilation. The airflow will then circulate to other areas of the house.

Ventilation out

Air exhausts are installed in the bathroom, kitchen, and toilets to remove moisture and unpleasant smells, as they are the rooms with "dirty" air.

Water

Rainwater non-drinkable & cold

This water has low calcium levels, making it ideal for watering plants and gardens. It can also be used for flushing toilets and washing machines, reducing the amount of soap required. While it doesn't need to be drinkable, it's still a valuable resource that shouldn't be wasted.

Water, Drinkable Both warm and cold

Used for daily water use.

Shower, Drinkable Both warm and cold

The mixing of warm-drink water and cold rainwater is not permitted when using water from a supply company, due to the high risk of flow back. However, the use of rainwater has benefits such as reducing the amount of water used, requiring less soap, and resulting in softer hair.

Drainage

Every water tap has of course a drain. But sometimes they come separate , like in the laundry room.

Toilets

You know what they do. Should be flushed with (filtered, non-stinky) rainwater.
