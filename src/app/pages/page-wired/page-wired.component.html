Smart home

Electricity

Normal - 230v

The standard in Sweden is 10amps per group (1.5mm2). In Netherlands that would be 16amps and 2.5mm2

3Phase - 400v

This weird symbol is a perilex plug. It has 3-phase (or sometimes just two). The main goal is the devide the load over 3 wires instead of one. It requires more wires though. Think induction in the kitchen, or e-car (not this connector).

lightning

Lamps

Lamps can come in 230v and 24v. Most lamps should be dimmable and wires run back to the PLC

Double switch

Double pulse switches. Allows for 6 options. Single, double and long press. They are 24v and wires run back to the PLC.

Dimmer

All dimmable lights have a dim level that became less bright during the evening. Following the circadian lightning.. This button is only to overrule and switch on or off

DLC, or light plugs

Completely strange for outstanders. but every fixed light has a seperate miniplug. In Sweden they had a old one, and since 2019 they follow the EU standard of DLC.

Network / Ethernet

Ethernet (rj45)

If you work home, you need reliable internet. A wall outlet for a normal internet cable works the best. Not limited to computers, many devices can have them as well. Like speakers, television etc.

Power-over-Ethernet

Its a internet cable with (often) 48volts. So you can power up a device with only the internet cable. Perfect for a ring-doorbel, security camera or even wifi-access-points

Wifi

Maybe most important for any mobile device, or guests

Safety

Smoke/heat detectors

We have a firefighter in the family. On a serious note, everyone should have multiple of smoke sensors. A heat sensor in the kitchen.

Camera's

The most optional step. Gives a bit of comfort, security wise. It does allow you to monitor your house, when you are away (via your own VPN of course)

Window/Door sensors/Motion detection

Extends the alarm functoin

Other smarts

Sun blinds / screens

Essential to a passive house, because the sun radiation is to much during the summer.

Motion sensors

Fun addon to help to automate the smart-home. Lights can be turned on/off automatically.

Temperature

Help to regulate the heat/cooling and ventilation in certain area's of the house

Ventilation

Ventilation in

Fresh air is needed in the bedrooms and main living rooms. It flows to the other parts of the house. Mainly because the air-intake in other rooms

Ventilation out

The air intakes are in the rooms with 'dirty' air. Of course the toilets, but also the bathroom/kitchen due to moisture and smells.
